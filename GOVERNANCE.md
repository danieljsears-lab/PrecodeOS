## Governance Model

PrecodeOS uses a benevolent founder-maintainer model.

Dan Sears / Recode sets project direction, accepts or rejects contributions, resolves roadmap disputes, and protects PrecodeOS's purpose: helping non-technical solo builders use AI coding agents with guided workflow, scope control, evidence, and human approval.

Private roadmap planning may exist in local files outside the public repo; official authority remains Dan Sears / Recode.

## Contribution Authority

Contributors may propose documentation, code, tests, examples, workflows, protocols, feedback issues, or package-bug issues when the maintainer has enabled an issue-intake path. Contribution and issue participation do not create governance rights, trademark rights, roadmap authority, maintainer status, merge rights, release authority, support obligations, or authority to represent official PrecodeOS.

GitHub Issues, labels, comments, pull requests, reviews, checks, and project boards are evidence until reviewed and promoted through the appropriate Precode owner file or maintainer decision. They do not approve PRDs, activate beads, choose roadmap direction, accept implementation, approve merge, approve package release, or replace maintainer review.

PrecodeOS™ and Precode™ are trademarks of Dan Sears / Recode. All trademark and brand rights are reserved under the project policy in `TRADEMARK.md`.

Maintainer review is required before any contribution changes:

- governance, contribution, trademark, license, or provenance policy
- public positioning or official project identity
- active memory rules
- authority ownership or promotion paths
- core workflow semantics
- generated-output demotion
- package install, update, setup, release-channel, or package-manager boundaries
- beginner-facing safety language

Trust-affecting semantic changes should use `tasks/reference/SEMANTIC-CHANGE-PROPOSAL-PROTOCOL.md` before implementation or merge. The proposal makes semantic risk visible for maintainer review; it does not delegate governance, approve implementation, publish releases, or create contributor decision rights.

## Forks And Succession

Forks are allowed under Apache-2.0. Forks and derivatives must follow `TRADEMARK.md` when naming or presenting themselves.

Official PrecodeOS direction, maintainer authority, and brand remain governed by Dan Sears / Recode unless maintainer authority or brand permission is explicitly delegated in writing.
